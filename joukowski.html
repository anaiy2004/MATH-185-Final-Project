Notes on implementation

Everything is computed in the z-plane and pushed through J point-by-point. The cylinder potential is closed-form, while the airfoil version needs a branch of J-1, which is a hassle near the cusp. Streamlines in the right panel are just streamlines from the left panel with J applied to each line segment.

Streamlines come from marching squares on a 240x200 grid of ψ values. (I tried RK4 streamline integration first but it was slow near stagnation points and the seed placement was annoying.) Saddle cells get disambiguated by the average of the four corners; not rigorous but fine in practice. Grid points inside the cylinder are NaN and any cell touching one is skipped, so the boundary takes care of itself.

Level values for ψ are spaced through sinh rather than uniformly, which puts more lines near ψ = 0 where the picture is most interesting. The log term in ψ blows up near z0, so I clip the range.

Kutta is just Γ = 4πUR sin(β − α) from the paper. As a check I evaluated F'(a) with that Γ and got ~10-17. Stagnation points come from sin(θ − α) = Γ/(4πUR); with Kutta on, one of them lands exactly at the cusp, which is the point.

Render is ~48k ψ evaluations per frame, debounced through requestAnimationFrame. Runs at 60fps on my laptop.
